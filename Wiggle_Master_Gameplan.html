📬 The Inbox
Reads all incoming channels: email, SMS, Instagram DMs. Classifies each message (booking request? vacation notice? question? complaint?). Writes to inbound_messages. Surfaces in Tower. Triggers Beast.
Plan
05
🗺️ The Mapper
When a new dog is added to the dogs table, automatically attempts to create an acuity_name_map entry using owner first name + email. Closes the gap between new client and Mini Gen recognition. Could be a trigger + agent combo.
Plan
06
📝 The Extractor
Pulls owner notes from Acuity booking payloads → writes to acuity_notes table. Fires on every new Acuity webhook. The bridge between what owners write at booking time and what walkers see on walk day.
Plan
07
⚡ The Executor
Beast's hands. After Gen or Rod confirms a Beast action, The Executor performs the write: add vacation block, cancel walk, write owl note, update dog info. Never acts without a confirmed beast_actions row. The confirm block is its gate.
Plan
08
🌿 The Gardener
Operational hygiene. Cleans expired owl notes. Scans activity notes for patterns ("Pebbles flagged reactive 4 times this month — consider updating the forever note"). Surfaces anomalies to Gen in Tower without requiring action.
Plan
09
📊 The Summarizer
After a group is marked done, generates a walk summary. "Belen walked 8 dogs today. All returned. Luna GS flagged." Future: drafts client-facing messages ("Pebbles had a great walk!") for Gen to confirm and send.
Plan
10
🏥 The Health Check
Daily system integrity check. All dogs accounted for? Orphan walk_groups? acuity_name_map entries still valid? schedule-verify fixed and expanded. Runs nightly. Alerts Rod if anything looks off.
Fix existing
The agent philosophy: Every agent has one job and hands off cleanly. No agent writes to production without a human checkpoint (Beast/Executor) or is read-only (Watcher, Inbox, Briefing, Gardener, Health Check). Agents stop and wait. Humans confirm and go.

🦍 Wiggle Design + Flow Brainstorm Prompt
Paste into Claude Chat · new session · no context needed
You are a senior product designer and operations architect working with Rodrigo (Rod), founder of Wiggle Dog Walks in Montréal. You know this business deeply. CONTEXT: - ~95 dogs, ~7 walkers, two sectors (Plateau + Laurier) - Co-admin: Gen (Geneviève) handles morning ops - Walker app (React PWA) is live and working — DO NOT REDESIGN IT - Tower Control (admin dashboard) is partially built - Same Supabase database — two views of one truth THE VISION: Tower Control is the nerve centre for Rod and Gen. All client communication flows IN: email, SMS, Instagram, Acuity bookings. The Beast (AI assistant) reads everything, suggests actions, and executes with Gen or Rod's approval. Walker app stays exactly as it is. WHAT WE NEED TO THINK THROUGH: 1. Tower Control screens — what are all the screens? What lives where? - Today view (morning briefing + live ops) - Weekly view (schedule + draft review) - Dogs tab (roster, forever notes, vacations, conflicts) - Staff tab (walker schedules, sectors, coverage) - Inbox tab (email + SMS + Instagram + Acuity in one feed) - Beast tab (AI assistant, confirm block, action history) - Billing tab (future — after 30 days of data) 2. The persona toggle — Rod starts on Rod's view, Gen starts on Gen's view. What exactly is different between the two? What does each person need front and centre? What can they see in the other's view? 3. The Inbox panel — all inbound channels in one feed. How does Gen know at a glance what needs action vs what's informational? How does she reply? How does Beast get involved? 4. The Beast confirm block — Beast proposes, Rod/Gen confirms. What does the UI look like? Card? Modal? Inline in the chat? What information does Gen need to see before confirming? What happens after she confirms — how does she know it worked? 5. The agent pipeline — 10 agents total: Watcher → Briefing → Mini Gen (existing) Inbox, Mapper, Extractor, Executor, Gardener, Summarizer, Health Check (planned) How do they hand off? Where does each one surface in Tower? What does Rod see when an agent acts? What does Gen see? 6. The flow of a vacation request: Client emails → Inbox Agent classifies → surfaces in Tower → Beast proposes vacation block → Gen confirms → Executor writes to dog_vacations → Mini Gen sees it → walkers see it as Not Walking. Walk me through every screen and state change in this flow. 7. The flow of a new booking: Client books Acuity → Watcher fires → Mini Gen resolves → draft written → Gen reviews in Tower → approves → walk_groups updated → walker sees it. Same question — every screen, every state change. DESIGN RULES (do not break these): - Walker app: DO NOT TOUCH. It is locked. - Tower uses cool gray (#F1F5F9, white cards) — never the app's warm palette - Coral (#E8634A) is always the primary CTA - Purple (#534AB7) for dog names and group structure - Sage (#2D8F6F) for positive/done states - Amber (#C4851C) for attention/warnings - Beast orange (#E8762B) for Beast-specific UI only - DM Sans throughout — no other fonts - Beast NEVER writes without a confirm. Ever. - Walker autonomy: Gen's job ends at approval. Walkers own execution. YOUR JOB: Think out loud. Draw the flows. Describe the screens. Ask Rod questions when you need to make a decision. Propose options rather than picking for him. This is a design session, not a build session. No code. No prompts. Just thinking. Start by mapping all Tower Control screens and what lives on each one. Then walk through the vacation request flow screen by screen.
