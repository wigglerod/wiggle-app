The foundation
Two surfaces. One truth.
Every decision in this product filters through four questions. They're not guidelines. They're a constitution. Break one and the whole thing breaks.
🧤
WWRS — What would the walker need right now?
One hand. Winter coat. A dog already pulling. If the feature requires two taps or reading a sentence, it fails this test. The walker's context is kinetic and cold. The app must be faster than the weather.
🏠
ONE PLACE — Every action has exactly one home.
Pickup happens in the drawer. Only the drawer. Owl notes happen in Tower and the note composer. Only there. When actions live in one place, walkers build muscle memory. When they don't, walkers make errors.
🪒
SIMPLEST — Strip it to the bone first.
The best feature is the one with the fewest moving parts that still solves the real problem. Occam's razor isn't a design preference — it's operational safety. Every extra tap in a hallway is a potential dropped dog.
🔄
BOTH VIEWS — Does it work in the app AND Tower?
A feature that only works in one view is incomplete. Gen writes an owl note in Tower — walkers see it in the app within seconds. A walker flags a dog — Gen sees it in Tower immediately. One database, two windows.
01
The card is a promise. Dog name, address, door code — visible without a single tap, no matter what layout mode the app is in. isCompact may reduce size. It may never remove content.
02
Nothing disappears. Not walking stays amber. Back home fades but remains. The count always matches reality. A disappearing card creates doubt — "did I miss one?" — and doubt at a door with six dogs is dangerous.
03
Every action is reversible. State is additive (insert) and reversible (delete). No destructive writes. Cold fingers misswipe. New walkers make mistakes. The cost of an error must be near zero.
04
Color is signal, not decoration. Purple on a name means "this dog has a forever note." Sage means picked up. Amber means needs attention. A color that does two jobs does neither — it becomes noise.
05
Beast never acts alone. Every AI suggestion requires a confirm block before executing. Mini Gen writes to staging, waits for Gen's approval. The agent stops. Gen decides. This is the only AI architecture Wiggle will ever use.

The Dog Profile Drawer — one dog, one place, everything.
Tap the dog's name. The drawer slides up. Every action that changes this dog's state for the day lives here — and nowhere else. Pickup, not walking, undo. Every piece of relevant information stacked in order of urgency.
  • ★ Forever Note (fuschia) → permanent standing instructions, admin-only edit, never expires
  • 🦉 Owl Note (amber) → timed staff-to-staff handoff, expires this week, sector-filtered
  • 📝 Activity (neutral) → full walk history from all walkers, permanent record
  • Auto-closes after status actions → picked up, back home, not walking, undo. App moves with the walker to the next door.
  • Stays open after info taps → editing times, reading notes. Never closes when the walker is reading.
The drawer is the control centre. If you're about to add a dog action somewhere other than the drawer — stop. That principle exists because walkers should never have to remember where things live.

The Note Composer — quick picks, one toggle, done.
After the walk. One tap on ✎ Note. Quick chips handle 90% of observations — no typing needed. The "Warn next walker" toggle does two things at once: writes the permanent activity record AND creates a timed owl note that walks on the dog's name card for three days.
  • Quick picks → tap a chip, hit Send. No keyboard, no winter glove removal.
  • 🚩 Flag chip → "something happened, ask me later." Zero typing. Gen sees it in Tower.
  • Warn next walker → dual-write: activity note (permanent) + owl note (3-day warning)
  • Free text → optional. Always. The chip is enough.
  • Every note carries → walker name, walker ID, timestamp. No anonymous mutations.
Four note types. Four lifetimes. ★ Forever never expires. 🦉 Owl lasts one week. 📅 Acuity expires today (Phase 2). 📝 Activity is the permanent record. The right note at the right time is the only note that helps.

Key tables — the single source of truth
walker_notes
ALL walk state lives here. pickup · returned · not_walking · group_done · note · resolver_flag. Walk_logs is empty — ignore it.
Every write carries walker_id, walker_name, walk_date. No anonymous mutations.
dogs.notes
Forever notes. 24 dogs have content today. When not null → dog name turns purple. Admin-only edit.
Never expires. The permanent standing instructions for every walk this dog will ever have.
owl_notes
Timed staff-to-staff notes. Expires weekly. HARD RULE: always filter by target_sector or notes bleed across sectors.
Acknowledged_by marks per-walker read status. Other walkers still see it until they read it.
walk_groups
Dog groups. dog_ids stores dog NAMES as text[] — not UUIDs. Intentional for debuggability. Unique constraint: (walk_date, group_num, sector).
Mini Gen writes to mini_gen_drafts first. Only moves to walk_groups after Gen approves.
mini_gen_drafts
Staging table for Mini Gen output. status: pending → approved → rejected. Never direct to production.
Gen approves via Tower. api/tower-approve.js promotes approved drafts to walk_groups.
acuity_name_map
77 entries. Bridges Acuity's owner-centric bookings to the dog-centric database. acuity_name = OWNER. dog_name = DOG.
Email disambiguates duplicate owner names. Same-home pairs get two rows. 100% resolution rate.
